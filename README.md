# Multi-cohort analysis identifies a blood-based immune transcriptomic signature for early lung cancer detection

Multicohort ANalysis of AggregaTed gEne Expression (MANATEE) is an analysis workflow designed to adapt our previously described multi-cohort analysis framework for broader analysis. We designed a modified MANATEE framework because there were not enough datasets containing samples from patients with lung cancer, and thus the typical MANATEE workflow was unable to effectively identify a diagnostic signature. We took 4 of our 5 lung cancer datasets and conormalized each one with over 50 other datasets. Next, each of these conormalized batches of data was randomly split into two evenly sized datasets. In addition, we split our fifth lung cancer dataset into 5 batches based on study site and technical platform. Thus, we had a total of 13 datasets containing both lung cancer samples and other samples. To select differentially expressed genes, we calculated and summarized differential expression statistics in each dataset and selected the top genes. Next, we performed a forward search wherein we continuously added which ever gene resulted in the largest increase in average AUROC across all 13 datasets.
